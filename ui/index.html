Win% = out of the last n times this exact setup fired in our backtest, the share that reached the target before the stop. It is a historical average, not a promise — any single trade can still lose.

Win% on its own doesn't tell you if a setup is good — it depends on your reward : risk (R:R). A bigger R:R means you can win less often and still come out ahead. The number to beat is the break-even Win%:

R:R 1 : 1
break-even 50%
R:R 1 : 1.5
break-even 40%
R:R 1 : 2
break-even 33%
R:R 1 : 3
break-even 25%

So yes — a 60% win at 1:2 is a strong edge: you only need 33% to break even, so 60% is well clear. Each trade is worth about +0.8R on average (risk 1 unit, net +0.8 over many trades).

Read the caveats: Win% is the backtested average over n trades — a bigger n is more trustworthy.
